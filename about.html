Robert's Story

This is the introductory header for the Robert's Story section

Robert has faced significant adversity and trauma throughout his life. Born two month's prematurely, in Reno, Nevada, Robert weighed blank, at birth, and was smaller than his father's hand. He spent his first months in an incubator.

At age nine, Robert was adopted by his former first grade teacher. Members of his extended family include a Pulitzer Prize winning writer, a former Ambassador and Diplomat.His biological great Grandfather John Duffy was a US Congressman, and New York State Supreme Court Justice.

At age thirteen, Robert's biological father Gordon C. Guerin died suddenly. His father was a US Navy Veteran, and also an Alaskan-Native.

At age fourteen, Robert was selected to serve as the youth cofounder of the Corvallis Youth Garden Project, a sustainable garden working with youth to grow and sell vegetables and herbs to local restaurants.

In 2004, Robert launched Prose Magazine (renamed Press) a hybrid digital and print, arts + culture + news magazine, but was unable to secure funding to scale the magazine.

In 2007, Robert launched Guerilla Division, a boutique web agency built around the guerilla marketing and direct marketing techniques of Jay Conrad Levinson, and Seth Godin, whom he had studied extensively as a teenager in Corvallis, OR.

In 2009, Robert was hired by the Corvallis Chamber of Commerce, interfacing Robert also worked with the Mid-Willamette Valley Chapter of the Technology Association of Oregon to repurpose the.

In October 2012, Robert started Log Camp, a nonprofit coding and computer science education program for diverse, underserved youth living in Multnomah and Washington Counties, OR.

Since January 2013, Robert has served as an unpaid Public Guardian for his mother, overseeing medical for his biological mother, a protected person suffering from extreme mental illness.
